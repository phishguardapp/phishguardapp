No Guarantee of Accuracy

PhishGuard uses multiple detection methods including AI analysis, domain age checking, SSL certificate validation, and content analysis. However:

  • No phishing detector is 100% accurate. Sophisticated phishing sites may evade detection.
  • False positives can occur. Legitimate sites may be flagged due to being new, using uncommon SSL providers, or containing certain keywords.
  • Detection takes time. Very new phishing sites may not be detected immediately.
  • Techniques evolve. Attackers constantly develop new methods to bypass detection.

Your Responsibility

You are ultimately responsible for your online safety. PhishGuard is a tool to assist you, not replace your judgment. You should:

  • Always verify URLs before entering sensitive information
  • Be skeptical of unsolicited emails, messages, or links
  • Never share passwords, credit card numbers, or personal information unless you're certain the site is legitimate
  • Use additional security measures like two-factor authentication
  • Keep your browser and operating system updated
  • Report suspicious sites to the appropriate authorities

Best Practices for Safe Browsing

  • Verify URLs carefully - Check for misspellings or unusual domains
  • Look for HTTPS - Legitimate sites use secure connections
  • Be wary of urgency - Phishing often creates false urgency
  • Use strong passwords - Unique passwords for each site
  • Enable 2FA - Two-factor authentication adds extra security
  • Trust your instincts - If something feels wrong, it probably is
  • Verify independently - Contact companies directly if you're unsure

If a Legitimate Site is Flagged

PhishGuard may occasionally flag legitimate sites. Common reasons include very new domains, uncommon SSL certificates, or certain keywords. If you trust a site, you can add it to your allowlist by clicking the extension icon and toggling "Trust this site". Only do this for sites you completely trust.
